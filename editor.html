The thesis

The default promise of AI is replacement. We think that's the wrong bet.

Cheaper content, fewer people, infinite output — and stories severed from their authors, their truth, and their intent. That's not the future creators, or the companies that depend on them, actually want.

The approach

Software built on three commitments.

Fuchsia exists to make the AI era better for storytelling, not poorer. Every product we build is held to the same standard.

01

Grounded in truth

Tools that respect the facts of a story and the world it depicts. Provenance and accuracy are features, not afterthoughts — so what audiences receive can be trusted.

02

Amplifies intention

We extend a creator's vision instead of overwriting it. The author stays the author — at every scale, on every platform, no matter how large the audience grows.

03

Enterprise by design

Backed by science, engineered for the world's biggest content companies, with UX worthy of the people who use it every day. Built to ship and to last.

What we're building

The Universal Character Model

An industry initiative to make story and character portable across platforms while retaining their creator's intent — even when they meet the agency of massive audiences. Developed in partnership with the offices of the CTOs at the studios shaping what comes next.

It's the first of the things we're building. More is in development.

Founder

Paul Bennun

Founder, Fuchsia

Paul is a British-American creative executive working where storytelling, technology, content and business coincide. He built Somethin' Else, a leading UK content studio that — on acquisition by Sony — was the largest supplier of interactive content to the BBC.

He has been a C-level executive at Los Angeles' KCRW and at British immersive studio Factory 42, making work for Meta, Disney and others. He is currently with USC's Entertainment Technology Center, building the future of storytelling for the Hollywood studios, and is an assessor for the UK government's innovation agency.

His work has won multiple BAFTAs and Cannes Lions, reached 96% on Metacritic, and featured talent including Sean Bean and Benedict Cumberbatch.
