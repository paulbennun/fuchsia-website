Getting started

What you need, and how to begin.

There's no account to create and no cloud project to set up. The core work runs entirely on your Mac.

What you need

A Mac with Apple silicon, running macOS 26. That's the whole list — there's no account to create, and the core work needs no internet connection.

Installing

From the Mac App Store. Download Fuchsia Studio, open it, and you're working: no sign-up, no login, no cloud project to create. You land in a native Mac document window and open — or start — an episode.

Price

$129, a one-time purchase on the Mac App Store — not a subscription. You buy Studio once and everything in it is yours; nothing in the base app is locked behind a higher tier.

Any sample rate

Bring it all in at any sample rate. A source already at the master rate passes through untouched — byte for byte; anything else is converted with mastering-grade resampling. Studio masters to the broadcast-standard 48 kHz.

Troubleshooting

Common questions.

Short, honest answers to the things producers ask most. If yours isn't here, email us — we read everything.

I've imported my tape — where is it?

Open Tape view (⌘3): your source is laid out there as a readable page, word for word. If a recording hasn't appeared yet, Studio is most likely still transcribing it and separating the speakers — all of that runs on your Mac, and hours of tape take real time to work through. It needs no account and no network connection; give it time.

A source shows as offline, or won't play.

The usual cause is that the audio lives on a drive that isn't connected. If you imported from an external drive that has since been unplugged, reconnect it — Studio needs to reach the original audio to play it.

The same applies at export: if export warns you about offline clips, moments in your running order point at audio Studio can't currently reach. Reconnect the drive that holds those recordings and export again.

The AI reads won't connect or aren't responding.

The reads — the Dramaturg, the interpretations in Reading view, and speaker-name proposals — are opt-in and run on your own AI provider account, not on your Mac. So first: have you added a provider key in Settings?

If a key is entered and the reads still won't run, check that you're online and that the key is still valid on the provider's side — an expired key or an exhausted allowance there will stop the reads. Everything else in Studio — import, transcription, speaker separation, search, editing, assembly, finishing — never uses the key and keeps working regardless.

A speaker label is wrong — can I correct it?

Yes. Speakers arrive as unnamed voices — Speaker 1, Speaker 2 — and you can rename any of them. A name you type yourself is never overwritten. If you've connected an AI provider, Studio proposes a name only when someone actually says it on tape: the moment plays, and nothing changes until you confirm.

One honest limit in this release: you can rename a voice, but not move a passage from one voice to another. If the separation itself put words under the wrong voice, we'd like to hear about it — get in touch so we know it matters to you.

How do I contact support or report a bug?

Email us at support@fuchsiaprojects.com. Tell us your macOS version and what you were doing when things went wrong; if it involves a specific recording, the file format and sample rate help too. We read everything, and feature requests are genuinely welcome.

Where can I read the privacy policy?

The short version, and the part that matters day to day: the craft — import, transcription, speaker separation, search, editing, finishing — runs on your Mac with no account, and your audio never leaves it. AI features are opt-in, run on your own provider key, and send only text, never your audio; material you mark as protected or inherited is blocked from ever leaving your machine.
