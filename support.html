About Fuchsia Studio

Fuchsia Studio is a macOS app for independent documentary and podcast producers. It makes your own archive of recorded tape askable — search everything you've recorded in plain language, land on the exact spoken moment, and hear the real audio instantly. From there it helps you pull moments into clips, shape a structure, draft and record narration in your own voice, and assemble a whole episode. The AI woven through it is designed to amplify your intent, never to replace it.

Getting started

  1. Choose your archive. Point Fuchsia Studio at the folder where your recordings live. It stays under your control — the app only accesses what you grant.
  2. Let it transcribe. Your audio is transcribed on-device and indexed so it becomes searchable, with playback synced to the words.
  3. Search and listen. Ask your archive in plain language, land on the exact moment, and hear the real tape.
  4. Assemble. Pull moments into clips, arrange them into a structure, draft narration, record your takes, and build the show.

Frequently asked questions

How much does it cost, and is there a trial?

Fuchsia Studio is $49 — a one-time purchase, not a subscription. You can try it first with a free trial: download the app for free, use it for the trial period, and unlock it permanently with a single in-app purchase when you're ready.

Does my audio ever leave my Mac?

No. Recording and transcription happen entirely on your device using Apple's on-device Speech framework. Your audio is never uploaded to us or to any third party. The only data that can leave your Mac is transcript text, and only if you choose to use AI assistance. See our Privacy Policy.

What do the AI features do, and where does my data go?

Optional AI assistance can suggest structure, draft narration, and answer questions about your material. When you use it, only transcript text — never your audio — is sent to the AI model provider you've configured. If you don't use these features, nothing leaves your device. You can keep everything on-device if you prefer.

How does transcription work, and which languages are supported?

Transcription uses Apple's built-in on-device speech recognition, which produces word-level timecodes so playback lines up with the text. The first time you transcribe, macOS downloads the speech model automatically. Language coverage follows Apple's on-device speech support for your region. If you work in a specific language and want to confirm support, get in touch.

Can it generate a presenter's or interviewee's voice?

No — by design. Any synthetic voice uses your own voice only, through Apple's Personal Voice, and only as a temporary preview to help you hear a show's shape before you record your real narration. Fuchsia Studio never clones the voice of a presenter, an interviewee, or anyone else.

Where is my work stored, and how do I back it up?

Your recordings, transcripts, and projects are stored locally in the folders you choose. Back them up the way you back up the rest of your Mac — for example, Time Machine or your preferred backup service. Because everything is local, you remain fully in control of your data.

The AI panel says it's offline. What should I check?

AI assistance needs a reachable model host. Check your internet connection and that the model provider you configured is available. If you use a self-hosted or local model, confirm it's running and reachable from your Mac. Without a reachable host, the app stays usable — it simply shows an honest "offline" state for AI features while everything on-device keeps working.

Transcription or microphone isn't working.

Open macOS System Settings → Privacy & Security and make sure Fuchsia Studio has permission for Microphone and Speech Recognition. If the first transcription stalls, confirm the on-device speech model finished downloading and try again. If it persists, email us with your macOS version and what you were doing.

Search returns nothing or misses a moment.

Make sure the relevant audio has finished transcribing and indexing — very long archives take time on first import. Try rephrasing in the words a speaker might actually have used. If you believe a moment should match and doesn't, let us know the query and we'll investigate.

How do I report a bug or request a feature?

Email support@fuchsiaprojects.com. Bug reports are most useful with your macOS version, the app version (below), and steps to reproduce. Feature requests are genuinely welcome — the roadmap is shaped by working producers.

How do I delete my data?

Your data lives in folders on your Mac, so you delete it like any other files. Removing your projects and uninstalling the app removes your data from your device. We hold no copy because we never receive it.
